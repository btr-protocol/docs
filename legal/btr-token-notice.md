# BTR TOKEN NOTICE

*Last Updated: May 2026*

## 1. PURPOSE OF THIS NOTICE

This notice describes the intended characteristics of the BTR token (the "BTR Token"). The BTR Token has not been issued at the date of this notice. All statements about the BTR Token, its distribution, its emissions schedule, and its governance role are **forward-looking** and subject to change, delay, or cancellation. Nothing in this notice constitutes an offer, solicitation, recommendation, or invitation to acquire any token, security, or financial instrument in any jurisdiction.

## 2. UTILITY AND GOVERNANCE FRAMING

The BTR Token is designed as a **utility and governance** token whose intended functions are:

- **Governance signal**: voting on parameter changes, treasury allocations, adapter onboarding, and protocol upgrades via the BTR governance process.
- **Staking utility (sBTR)**: locking BTR to obtain a non-transferable governance position with conviction-weighted voting power.
- **Fee discounting** (forward-looking): potential routing or fee-tier benefits within BTR-operated front-ends, subject to governance approval.

The BTR Token is **not** designed or intended to:

- represent equity, debt, or any claim on the assets, revenue, or profits of any legal entity;
- entitle holders to dividends, distributions, redemptions, or any fixed or variable yield;
- represent a deposit or any insured banking instrument;
- function as a payment token or a means of exchange outside the BTR protocol context.

## 3. NO-OFFER, NO-SECURITY POSITION

BTR (the project) takes the position that the BTR Token, as designed, is not a security under the laws of the Cayman Islands, the project's home jurisdiction. BTR makes no representation as to the classification of the BTR Token under the laws of any other jurisdiction.

### Characterization: utility token, not security

BTR is structured as a utility/governance token. Considerations:

1. **No common enterprise with profit expectation managed by BTR Foundation.** Token-holders' returns depend on protocol-wide outcomes (fee generation, governance decisions, market conditions); the Foundation does not undertake to generate returns for token-holders.
2. **Staking rewards are protocol-distributed fee-share for governance participation**, not passive income. The "rewards" terminology used elsewhere in BTR docs refers to (a) emission share for active staking, (b) treasury fee share. These are protocol-mechanism outputs, not Foundation-managed yield.
3. **No fiduciary obligation.** The Foundation does not act as agent or fiduciary for token-holders (see [Terms of Service §14](./terms-of-service.md) and [Risk Disclaimer §8](./risk-disclaimer.md)).
4. **Decentralized governance.** Protocol parameters are set via on-chain governance with timelock; no single party (including the Foundation) has unilateral parameter control.
5. **Jurisdictional carve-out.** This characterization is jurisdiction-dependent. US users should consult counsel re: the Howey test (SEC v. W.J. Howey, 328 U.S. 293 (1946)) and recent SEC litigation; EU/UK/SG users re: their local frameworks. BTR Foundation does not represent that BTR is not a security in any specific jurisdiction.
