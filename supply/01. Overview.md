# Overview

> BTR Supply is the protocol's ALM (Automated Liquidity Management) layer. Users deposit single-asset capital into vaults; the vault routes capital across adapters (BTR DEX pools + 3rd-party CL DEX pools) and rebalances per strategy.

**Automated Liquidity Management** -yield-bearing single-asset ERC-4626/7540 vaults that allocate to external concentrated-liquidity DEX pools (UniV3/V4, PancakeV3/Infinity, Algebra, Ramses, Aerodrome Slipstream) and, forward-looking, BTR's own AIMM pools.

---

## 1. Position in the Product Stack

BTR ships two complementary primitives:

| Product | Role | Code anchor |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **BTR DEX (AIMM)** | BTR's own AMM -anchor-path-priced, inventory-aware multi-asset pools | `dex/contracts/src/` |
| **BTR Supply (ALM Vaults)** | Single-asset deposit → automated allocation across external CLMM/DLMM pools and (future) AIMM pools | `alm/evm/src/` |

A user holding `USDC` deposits to a vault, receives fungible vault shares (ERC-4626/7540), and earns net-of-fee yield from concentrated LP positions managed by the protocol. The vault chooses ranges, rebalances atomically or via ERC-7683 intents, and crystallizes performance fees on a high-water mark.

## 2. Mental Model

- **Earn-first surface**. Vaults are the passive yield product; Swap (AIMM) is the active trading product. The two share the same treasury, governance, and price-feed substrate.
- **Single-asset accounting**. Every vault is denominated in one ERC-20 (`Vault.sol:37 _asset`). LP exposure is internal to the adapter; users see only one decimal of risk: the deposit asset.
- **Worst-of marks**. Share price is pessimistic -at each adapter, `min(pool, oracle)` denominated in `asset`. JIT-extraction cost ≈ pool slippage; oracle-extraction cost ≈ Chainlink staleness. Never both.
