# Labels


### §4.1 General framing

Supervised learning needs a target $y_t$ paired with feature vector $\mathbf{x}_t$. For gradient-boosted decision trees (GBM), labels are the prediction target the trees split on: the loss minimized at each node is computed against $y_t$. The choice of label entirely determines what the model learns; features are merely the substrate from which the target is approximated.

In BTR Prime three label families coexist, all derived from a forward window of $H$ bars (`horizon_bars`, default 20):

1. **Directional binary** $y_t \in \lbrace 0, 1\rbrace$ - used by LO and SO classifiers.
2. **Parkinson-normalized regression** $y_t \in [-5, +5]$ - used when `use_regression = true`.
3. **CL net-α** $y_t \in \lbrace 0,1\rbrace$ (binary) or $[-0.05, 0.05]$ (regression) - used by the LP range-management classifier.

A single trained model is always **one-sided**: a LO model and an SO model are trained separately on disjoint label definitions (line 460, `crates/ml/src/trainer.rs`). The architecture explicitly forbids a unified $\lbrace -1, 0, +1\rbrace$ label because mode-specific feature interactions differ (e.g. upside breakout features differ from downside-cascade features).

### §4.2 Directional label (LO / SO)

**File:line refs:** `crates/ml/src/features/labels.rs:10-32`; `crates/ml/src/trainer.rs:456-476`.

Let $C_t$ denote the close price of bar $t$ and let the forward log-style return over horizon $H$ be

$$
r_{t,t+H} \;=\; \frac{C_{t+H}}{C_t} - 1.
$$

Let $\tau$ denote `label_threshold` (config `label_threshold: 0.001`, i.e. 10 bps). The directional label is

$$
y_t^{\text{LO}} \;=\;
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } r_{t,t+H} > \tau, \\
0 & \text{otherwise},
\end{cases}
\qquad
y_t^{\text{SO}} \;=\;
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } r_{t,t+H} < -\tau, \\
0 & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Tail bars where $t + H \ge n$ produce `None` and are excluded from training (`labels.rs:19`). Bars with non-positive price are likewise dropped.

The trainer collapses both modes into a single sign-flipped expression (`trainer.rs:460`):

$$
\tilde r_t = \begin{cases} -r_{t,t+H} & \text{if mode = "so"} \\ r_{t,t+H} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}, \qquad y_t = \mathbf{1}\lbrace \tilde r_t > \tau\rbrace .
$$

#### Why the threshold is fixed and decoupled

`label_threshold = 0.001` is **not** optimized by CMA-ES, and **not** identical to the trade-entry conviction threshold `entry_threshold_sigma`. The rationale, encoded as project memory ("Label thr decoupled"):

- The label answers a measurement question: *was there a directional move large enough that microstructure noise didn't dominate?* 10 bps is roughly one tick on liquid FX/crypto majors at M15 cadence - below this, the sign of $r_{t,t+H}$ is dominated by bid–ask bounce, not by signal.
- The conviction question - *should we trade?* - is answered by the stage-2 meta-classifier (and/or `entry_threshold_sigma` in regression mode) which can demand far stronger evidence than 10 bps before committing capital.
- Coupling label and conviction creates a degenerate optimization surface: increasing $\tau$ both shrinks the positive class (label problem) **and** raises the trade bar (selection problem), which CMA-ES cannot disentangle. Pinning $\tau$ small fixes the labeling at "is there any directional information at all?" and leaves trade selection as a separable downstream problem.

#### Regression variant

When `use_regression = true` (`trainer.rs:461-468`), labels become Parkinson-normalized scaled forward returns:

$$
y_t^{\text{reg}} \;=\; \mathrm{clip}\left(\frac{\tilde r_t}{\sigma_P(t)\,\sqrt{H}},\; -5,\; +5\right),
$$

where $\sigma_P(t)$ is a rolling Parkinson volatility on a `parkinson_lookback = 50`-bar window. This places labels on a unit-variance scale: $y_t \approx \pm 1$ corresponds to a one-standard-deviation move over the horizon. The downstream entry rule then becomes "predict $\hat y_t > k$" where $k =$ `entry_threshold_sigma`, i.e. demand a $k$-σ predicted move (`trainer.rs:580-586`).

### §4.3 Triple-barrier method

The triple-barrier method of López de Prado (2018, ch. 3) - labelling by which of {upper PT barrier, lower SL barrier, vertical time barrier} a path hits first - does **not** appear in `labels.rs` or `trainer.rs`. Prime uses only the fixed-horizon forward return. The implicit trade-off:

- **Fixed horizon (current):** labels are i.i.d.-ish in time-of-issue, easy to compute, and align cleanly with information-bar cadence (DIB bars are already approximately equal-information, so a fixed-bar horizon ≈ a fixed-information horizon).
- **Triple-barrier (not used):** would yield labels that better match path-dependent realized P&L (a TP/SL is what we actually exit on), at the cost of (a) overlapping observations needing concurrency weights, and (b) path-dependent leakage between SL/TP placement and σ estimates. Given that the GBM ensemble already uses Parkinson σ for SL/TP at the *trading* stage, encoding the same barriers at the *label* stage would double-count the volatility channel.

### §4.4 Meta-labels (López de Prado two-stage)

**File:line refs:** `crates/ml/src/trainer.rs:1-3` (doc header), `:203-207` (`FoldModels { primary_models, meta_model: Option<GBMModel> }`), `:587, :888`.

The architecture is explicitly two-stage:

> Stage 1 (Primary): Binary classifier predicts favorable direction.
> Stage 2 (Meta):    Binary classifier filters false positives + calibrates bet size.

**Stage 1 label.** As §4.2: $y_t^{(1)} = \mathbf{1}\lbrace \tilde r_t > \tau\rbrace$. The primary GBM is trained on $(\mathbf{x}_t, y_t^{(1)})$ over the rolling training window.

**Stage 2 label.** Defined on the *subset* of bars where the primary fires ($\hat y_t^{(1)} = 1$ or $\hat p_t^{(1)} >$ threshold). For each fired bar $t$, simulate the bet that would have been placed at $t$ with horizon $H$ and the configured SL/TP, subtract round-trip costs $c$, and label

$$
y_t^{(2)} \;=\;
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } \pi_t > 0, \\
0 & \text{otherwise},
\end{cases}
\quad
\pi_t \;=\; (\tilde r_{t,\text{exit}} - c) \cdot \mathbf{1}\lbrace \hat y_t^{(1)} = 1\rbrace .
$$

The meta classifier's predicted probability $\hat p_t^{(2)}$ is then used to (a) suppress trades where the primary is "right direction but unprofitable after costs" and (b) scale bet size (in production, Kelly-fraction-style). Crucially $y_t^{(2)} = 0$ when the primary did not fire (so meta only learns to filter, never to invent trades).

**Implementation status.** The trainer plumbs the meta-model slot (`meta_model: Option<GBMModel>` in `FoldModels`) but the current `walk_forward_signals_labeled` path leaves it `None` (`trainer.rs:587, :888`). Stage 2 is wired-but-not-populated; pulling in the meta-label generator (forward-simulated P&L over SL/TP barriers minus costs) is a documented next step.

### §4.5 CL labels - net α vs HODL

**File:line refs:** `crates/ml/src/cl.rs:96-198` (`compute_cl_labels`).

The CL classifier predicts *whether a centered, narrow LP position will out-earn buy-and-hold over the next $H$ bars*. The label is the realized **net α**:

$$
\alpha^{\text{CL}}_t \;=\; \frac{\mathrm{LP}_{t,t+H}}{\mathrm{HODL}_{t,t+H}} - 1,
$$

where the two terms are unitless multiplicative factors over the horizon. Concretely (cl.rs:130–186):

**Buy-and-hold factor:**

$$
\mathrm{HODL}_{t,t+H} \;=\; \frac{P_{t+H}}{P_t}.
$$

**LP factor.** A position is opened centered at $P_t$ with half-width $w =$ `default_half_width`, giving range $[P_\ell, P_h] = [P_t(1-w),\, P_t(1+w)]$. Let $L(P, P_\ell, P_h)$ denote the Uniswap-v3 LP value function `lp_value`. Then

$$
\mathrm{LP}_{t,t+H} \;=\; \underbrace{\frac{L(P_{t+H}, P_\ell, P_h)}{L(P_t, P_\ell, P_h)}}_{\text{impermanent value (IL)}} \cdot \underbrace{\prod_{j=t+1}^{t+H} \big(1 + f_j\big)\big(1 - \ell_j\big)}_{\text{fees} \times \text{LVR}}.
$$

**Per-bar fee accrual** (cl.rs:137–156, active only when $P_j \in [P_\ell, P_h]$):

$$
f_j \;=\; \frac{\text{base-apr}}{N} \cdot v_j \cdot c_j,
$$

with $N$ = `bars_per_year`, concentration

$$
c_j \;=\; \min\left(\frac{L(P_j,\, P_j(1-w_{\text{ref}}),\, P_j(1+w_{\text{ref}}))}{L(P_j, P_\ell, P_h)},\; 3\right),
$$

vol-fee multiplier (with single-bar Parkinson $\sigma_j = |\ln(H_j/L_j)| / \sqrt{4\ln 2}$ and cadence-scaled reference $\sigma_{\text{ref}} = 0.0013\sqrt{35064/N}$):

$$
v_j \;=\; \mathrm{clip}\left(\bigl(\sigma_j / \sigma_{\text{ref}}\bigr)^{0.7},\; v_{\text{floor}},\; 2\right).
$$

The exponent 0.7 is the empirical Uniswap-v3 elasticity of fees with respect to vol (sublinear: doubling vol does not double fees because reach also widens).

**Per-bar LVR drag** (cl.rs:160–167), from Milionis et al. 2022:

$$
\ell_j \;=\; \frac{\sigma_{j,\text{ann}}^2 \cdot \sqrt{P_j}}{8 \cdot L(P_j, P_j(1-w_{\text{ref}}), P_j(1+w_{\text{ref}}))} \cdot c_j \cdot \frac{1}{N},
\qquad \sigma_{j,\text{ann}} = 0.75\,\sigma_j \sqrt{N}.
$$

The 0.75 dampening factor strips Parkinson microstructure bias (H/L includes bid–ask bounce); combined with the correct 1/8 LVR constant this yields effective LVR ≈ 56% of theoretical, matching empirical fee/LVR ratios of 1.5–3.0× on majors.

**Binary CL label.** With `is_regression = false` (cl.rs:188–192):

$$
y_t^{\text{CL}} \;=\; \mathbf{1}\bigl\lbrace \alpha^{\text{CL}}_t > \tau\bigr\rbrace ,
\qquad \tau = \text{label-threshold}.
$$

**Regression CL label:**

$$
y_t^{\text{CL,reg}} \;=\; \mathrm{clip}(\alpha^{\text{CL}}_t,\; -0.05,\; +0.05).
$$

The ±5% clip prevents extreme single-period outliers (e.g. de-pegs) from dominating the L2 loss. Tail bars and bars with $L(P_t, P_\ell, P_h) < 10^{-18}$ are `None`.

**Why net-α and not raw LP return.** A model that predicts raw LP return is dominated by the price-direction signal: when $P_{t+H} > P_t$ the LP rises mechanically with HODL. Subtracting HODL removes the directional confound and forces the model to learn what is specific to LP economics - namely *when does fee accrual exceed IL + LVR?* This is the right question for an LP range-management policy, which cannot influence price but can choose width and rebalance timing. The label is, in causal-inference terms, a difference-in-differences signal: CL return minus the counterfactual no-LP return.

### §4.6 Sample weights and class balance

The current codebase does **not** implement a `compute_sample_weights` function. The trainer passes labels straight to `GBMModel::train_flat` without per-sample weights (`trainer.rs:562, :875`). The implicit weighting scheme is uniform: every valid label contributes equally to the loss.

Two related mechanisms partially substitute for sample weighting:

1. **Train-stride subsampling** (`trainer.rs:499-502`). Each fold targets at most ≈7 000 training rows; if the training window exceeds this, a `train_stride = train_bars / max_train_rows` is applied, taking every $k$-th bar. This is uniform decimation, not importance weighting, but it does enforce a soft upper bound on training cost and keeps fold-to-fold sample sizes comparable.

2. **Ensemble decay across folds** (`trainer.rs:651-653`). At prediction time, older folds in `fold_history` receive weight $\rho^{\text{age}}$ with $\rho =$ `ensemble_decay`. This is *prediction-time* exponential downweighting of stale models - the equivalent at the label-aggregation layer - and serves the same purpose a Lopez-de-Prado time-decay sample weight would: more recent regime evidence dominates.

**Class balance.** With $\tau = 0.001$ and reasonably symmetric forward-return distributions, the positive class share on directional labels is empirically ≈ 25–35% on M15 data. No explicit class re-weighting is applied; the LightGBM-style histogram learner copes with mild imbalance through leaf-wise growth, and the label distribution diagnostic at `trainer.rs:478-490` exists precisely to surface pathological imbalance for human review rather than to auto-correct it.

**Concurrency weights.** López de Prado's concurrency weighting (down-weighting overlapping labels because samples $t, t+1, \dots, t+H-1$ share part of their forward window) is also not implemented. The fixed-horizon design produces overlapping labels by construction; this is a known under-weighting of effective sample size that biases CV estimates of generalization optimistically. Mitigations in the current design: (a) the embargo `horizon_bars + eval_interval` between train and predict windows breaks the most acute leakage (`trainer.rs:495`), and (b) the walk-forward fold structure ensures no future bar leaks into any training set. A López de Prado-style concurrency-weighted sample weight would be the natural next addition once meta-labels are activated.
