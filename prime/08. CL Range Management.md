# CL Range Management


### §8.1 Problem statement

A Uniswap V3-style concentrated-liquidity (CL) liquidity provider (LP) commits capital to a finite price interval $[p_l, p_h]$ on an automated market maker (AMM). The instantaneous value of the position is a convex function of the marginal price $p$, and the position accrues swap fees only while $p \in [p_l, p_h]$. The provider faces two adversarial flows: (i) impermanent loss (IL), which is the path-dependent shortfall of $V_{\text{LP}}(p)$ relative to a passive 50/50 holding of the underlying basket, and (ii) loss-versus-rebalancing (LVR), the arbitrage rent extracted by informed traders against a stale on-chain quote (Milionis, Moallemi, Roughgarden, Zhang 2022).

Let the horizon be $H$ bars. Given the current price $p_t$ and a vector of side information $x_t \in \mathbb{R}^d$, the optimization problem is

$$
(p_l^\star, p_h^\star) \;=\; \arg\max_{(p_l, p_h)} \;\; \mathbb{E}\left[\,R^{\text{LP}}_{t,t+H}(p_l, p_h) \;-\; R^{\text{HODL}}_{t,t+H}\,\Big|\, x_t\right]
$$

subject to operational constraints: rebalance gas $g$, swap fee $\phi_s$, tick spacing $\tau$, and a minimum-improvement throttle. The Prime pipeline reuses the perpetual gradient-boosted decision tree (GBDT) booster that drives LO/SO directional prediction, but rotates both the *label* (continuous net CL alpha vs. HODL, or its discretized sign) and the *backtest* (continuous LP rather than discrete trades).

### §8.2 Range parameterization

Inspection of `prime_core::range` and `prime_ml::cl` reveals a hybrid parameterization. The `compute_range` family (used by the deterministic force-driven baseline) employs an **additive symmetric-with-bias** form keyed on a *vol-force-driven width*:

$$
w(\,\text{vf}\,) \;=\; w_{\min} + (w_{\max} - w_{\min})\;\cdot\;\Bigl[\,1 - \exp\bigl(-\alpha_v\,\text{vf}/\beta_v\bigr)\Bigr]_0^1
$$

where $\text{vf} \in [0, 100]$ is the volatility force, $\alpha_v = -\text{vforce\_exp}$, $\beta_v = \text{vforce\_divider}$. The bracket $[\cdot]_0^1$ denotes clamping to $[0,1]$. Low volatility collapses width to $w_{\min}$ (concentrated, high fee density); high volatility expands to $w_{\max}$ (defensive against LVR).

The signed width is then *split asymmetrically by trend bias* $b_t \in [-1, 1]$:

$$
r_{\text{min}} \;=\; w \cdot p_t \cdot \frac{1}{1 + |b_t|}, \qquad
r_{\text{max}} \;=\; w \cdot p_t \cdot (1 + |b_t|)
$$

with the two assignments swapped when $b_t < 0$, yielding

$$
p_l \;=\; p_t - r_{\text{min}}, \qquad p_h \;=\; p_t + r_{\text{max}} \quad\text{(bullish bias)}.
$$

The ML CL path in `compute_cl_labels` instead uses a **multiplicative symmetric** form keyed on a *fraction-of-price* half-width $\eta$:

$$
p_l \;=\; p_t\,(1 - \eta), \qquad p_h \;=\; p_t\,(1 + \eta), \qquad \eta = \text{default\_half\_width}.
$$

ML signals modulate $\eta$ by discrete multipliers:

$$
\eta_t \;=\; \eta_0 \cdot \begin{cases} \kappa_{\text{tight}} & s_t = +1 \\ 1 & s_t = 0 \\ \kappa_{\text{wide}} & s_t = -1 \end{cases}
\qquad (\kappa_{\text{tight}} = 0.6,\;\kappa_{\text{wide}} = 1.8).
$$

Translating this into the canonical $\sigma$-normalized form preferred for cross-asset transfer, with Parkinson per-bar vol $\hat\sigma_P$ and horizon $H$:

$$
\eta \;\;\overset{!}{=}\;\; w \cdot \hat\sigma_P \sqrt{H}
\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad p_l = p_t\,e^{-w\,\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}},\;\; p_h = p_t\,e^{+w\,\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}}
$$

to first order in $w\,\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}$. This is the canonical parameterization targeted by the booster: predict dimensionless $\sigma$-multiples and recover prices by exponentiating against the locally-estimated diffusion budget.

### §8.3 CL math primitives

**Tick representation.** Uniswap V3 discretizes the log-price axis into ticks of base 1.0001:

$$
T \;=\; \log_{1.0001}(p), \qquad p \;=\; 1.0001^{T}.
$$

Pool fee tiers carry a tick spacing $\tau \in \lbrace 1, 10, 60, 200\rbrace$; valid range endpoints satisfy $T_l, T_h \in \tau\mathbb{Z}$. The Prime helper `align_tick` rounds the raw lower tick *down* and the raw upper tick *up* using Euclidean division (cf. `range.rs:104-118`), guaranteeing the ML-chosen interval is a superset of the floating-point target:

$$
T_l^{\text{aligned}} \;=\; \tau\,\bigl\lfloor T_l / \tau \bigr\rfloor, \qquad
T_h^{\text{aligned}} \;=\; \tau\,\bigl\lceil T_h / \tau \bigr\rceil.
$$

**Liquidity invariant.** For a constant-product pool with virtual reserves bounded to $[p_l, p_h]$, the in-range invariant per unit liquidity $L$ is (Adams, Zinsmeister, Salem, Keefer, Robinson 2021, Eqs. 6.29–6.30):

$$
x \;=\; L\,\frac{\sqrt{p_h} - \sqrt{p}}{\sqrt{p_h}\sqrt{p}}, \qquad
y \;=\; L\,\bigl(\sqrt{p} - \sqrt{p_l}\bigr), \qquad p \in [p_l, p_h].
$$

Out-of-range positions collapse to single-sided inventory:

$$
p \le p_l:\quad x = L\left(\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{p_l}} - \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{p_h}}\right),\; y=0;
\qquad
p \ge p_h:\quad x = 0,\; y = L\left(\sqrt{p_h} - \sqrt{p_l}\right).
$$

**Value function (token1-denominated).** The position value $V(p; p_l, p_h) = x\cdot p + y$ per unit $L$, as implemented in `lp_value` (`range.rs:74-84`):

$$
v(p; p_l, p_h) \;=\;
\begin{cases}
p\,\bigl(p_l^{-1/2} - p_h^{-1/2}\bigr) & p \le p_l \\[4pt]
2\sqrt{p} - \sqrt{p_l} - p\,p_h^{-1/2} & p_l < p < p_h \\[4pt]
\sqrt{p_h} - \sqrt{p_l} & p \ge p_h
\end{cases}
$$

The middle branch is the centerpiece of all CL P&L accounting in `compute_cl_labels` and `backtest_cl_signals`. It is concave in $\sqrt{p}$, capturing exactly the convexity drag that produces IL.

### §8.4 LP value function and impermanent loss

Let $p_0, p_1$ be the entry and exit prices and let the position be deployed in $[p_l, p_h]$ with $p_0 \in [p_l, p_h]$. Holding $L$ fixed (no fees, no rebalancing), the impermanent loss vs. the 50/50 rebalanced HODL benchmark is

$$
\text{IL}^{\text{CL}}(p_1; p_0, p_l, p_h) \;=\; \frac{v(p_1; p_l, p_h)}{v(p_0; p_l, p_h)} \cdot \frac{V_0^{\text{HODL}}}{V_0^{\text{HODL}}\cdot \tfrac12(1 + p_1/p_0)} \;-\; 1.
$$

For a **full-range** position ($p_l \to 0$, $p_h \to \infty$) this reduces to the textbook constant-product IL:

$$
\text{IL}_{\infty}(\rho) \;=\; \frac{2\sqrt{\rho}}{1 + \rho} - 1, \qquad \rho \equiv \tfrac{p_1}{p_0},
$$

which is non-positive with minimum at $\rho = 1$. For a **concentrated** position, IL is amplified by a *capital-efficiency leverage* factor. Define the geometric mid $p_g = \sqrt{p_l p_h}$ and the half-width ratio $r = \sqrt{p_h/p_l}$. Following Adams et al. (2021, §6.2), the same $L$ that one would deploy full-range maps onto a centered concentrated position with effective leverage

$$
\mathcal{L}(r) \;=\; \frac{1}{1 - r^{-1/2}} \;\approx\; \frac{2\sqrt{r}}{r-1} \quad\text{(narrow-range limit)},
$$

so for small $\rho - 1$ the local IL satisfies

$$
\text{IL}^{\text{CL}} \;\approx\; \mathcal{L}(r)\cdot \text{IL}_{\infty}(\rho) \;\approx\; -\frac{1}{8}\,\mathcal{L}(r)\,(\rho - 1)^2 + O\bigl((\rho-1)^3\bigr).
$$

**LVR derivation in the CL-with-reference form.** The classical Milionis et al. (2022, Theorem 1) identity for the instantaneous rebalancing rent paid by a continuously-arbitraged AMM position is

$$
\ell_{\text{LVR}}^{\text{full}}(t) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\sigma^2(t)\,p(t)\,\mathcal{P}''(p(t)),
$$

where $\mathcal{P}(p)$ is the pool-value-function (PVF) of the AMM and primes denote derivatives in $p$. For a Uniswap-V3 in-range position with unit liquidity $L = 1$, the token1-denominated PVF is the middle branch of $v(p; p_l, p_h)$ in §8.3, whose second derivative evaluates to

$$
v''(p; p_l, p_h) \;=\; -\,\tfrac{1}{2}\,p^{-3/2}\qquad (p_l < p < p_h).
$$

Substituting yields the per-unit-liquidity in-range LVR rate

$$
\ell_{\text{LVR}}^{(L=1)}(t) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\sigma^2(t)\,p(t)\cdot \tfrac{1}{2}\,p(t)^{-3/2} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{4}\,\sigma^2(t)\,p(t)^{-1/2}.
$$

The Prime label code does **not** report rent per unit $L$; it reports rent as a *fractional drag on the position's own dollar value* $\mathcal{V}_{\text{pos}} = L\cdot v(p; p_{l,\text{pos}}, p_{h,\text{pos}})$, with $L$ pinned by a *reference* (narrow-width) position $L_{\text{ref}} \equiv v(p; p_{l,\text{ref}}, p_{h,\text{ref}})$ that anchors the fee-concentration computation in §8.5. Re-expressing the rent as a rate-per-dollar of reference liquidity gives

$$
\ell_{\text{LVR}}^{\text{CL}}(t) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{8}\,\sigma^2(t)\,\frac{\sqrt{p(t)}}{L_{\text{ref}}(t)} \cdot \mathbf{1}\lbrace p(t) \in [p_l, p_h]\rbrace ,
$$

where the factor $\tfrac{1}{8}$ absorbs $\tfrac{1}{4}$ from the second derivative together with a further $\tfrac{1}{2}$ from the $\sqrt{p}$ Jacobian when integrating the rent against $d\sqrt{p}$ rather than $dp$ (this is the standard Milionis–Moallemi–Roughgarden continuous-time identity rewritten in $\sqrt{p}$ coordinates, the natural CL coordinate per §8.3). The $\sqrt{p}/L_{\text{ref}}$ argument structure - as opposed to the $p\cdot L$ structure one sees in the full-range or "rent per unit $L$" presentation - reflects two CL-specific adaptations: (i) $L$ is now a *dimensional* quantity tied to a chosen range and is therefore divided out by the reference, and (ii) the position's quadratic loss in $\sqrt{p}$-space scales as the local concavity of $v$, which carries one factor of $p^{-1/2}$ rather than $p^{+1}$.

This is the exact constant invoked in the Prime label code at `crates/ml/src/cl.rs:163`:

```rust
let lvr_base = sigma_annual * sigma_annual * 0.125 * price.sqrt() / lp_ref;
```

A Parkinson-microstructure dampening factor `LVR_VOL_DAMPENING = 0.75` is applied to $\sigma$ *before* squaring (`cl.rs:160-161`):

$$
\sigma_{\text{LVR}}(t) \;=\; 0.75 \cdot \hat\sigma_P(t), \qquad \ell_{\text{LVR}}^{\text{Prime}}(t) \;=\; 0.75^{2}\cdot \tfrac{1}{8}\,\hat\sigma_P^{2}(t)\,\frac{\sqrt{p(t)}}{L_{\text{ref}}(t)} \;\approx\; 0.5625 \cdot \ell_{\text{LVR}}^{\text{CL}}(t).
$$

This $\approx 44\%$ reduction corrects the Parkinson high-low estimator's known upward bias from bid-ask bounce and discrete-time aliasing, both of which inflate the realized $\sigma^2$ used in the rent calculation. The remaining in-range indicator $\mathbf{1}\lbrace p \in [p_l, p_h]\rbrace$ is enforced operationally in the same loop by the gating check at `cl.rs:140` (computing $L_{\text{ref}}$, $L_{\text{pos}}$ only for in-range bars).

### §8.5 Fee accrual and the width-concentration trade-off

Let $\phi$ be the pool fee tier, $V_t$ the per-bar dollar volume, and $L_{\text{us}}/L_{\text{tot}}$ our share of *in-range* liquidity. Fees accrue at instantaneous rate

$$
\dot f_t \;=\; \phi \cdot V_t \cdot \frac{L_{\text{us}}}{L_{\text{tot}}(p_t)} \cdot \mathbf{1}\lbrace p_t \in [p_l, p_h]\rbrace .
$$

For a fixed dollar position, $L_{\text{us}}$ scales as the inverse of the *area* of the range in $\sqrt{p}$-space. Prime models this via a **fee-concentration multiplier** anchored at a reference half-width $\eta_{\text{ref}} = 0.07$:

$$
\mathcal{C}(\eta) \;=\; \min\left(\frac{v(p; p_t(1-\eta_{\text{ref}}), p_t(1+\eta_{\text{ref}}))}{v(p; p_t(1-\eta), p_t(1+\eta))},\; \mathcal{C}_{\max}\right), \qquad \mathcal{C}_{\max} = 3.
$$

The cap encodes the empirical Uniswap V3 observation that fees do not scale linearly past $\sim 3\times$ concentration due to competition from other tight LPs. The per-bar fee delta becomes (cf. `cl.rs:147-156`):

$$
\Delta f \;=\; \frac{\text{APR}_0 \cdot \mu_\sigma \cdot \mathcal{C}(\eta)}{B_y}, \qquad
\mu_\sigma \;=\; \exp\bigl(0.7 \cdot \ln(\hat\sigma_P / \sigma_{\text{ref}})\bigr) \;=\; \bigl(\hat\sigma_P/\sigma_{\text{ref}}\bigr)^{0.7}
$$

clamped to $[\mu_{\min}, 2]$, with $B_y$ bars per year. The vol-fee exponent $0.7$ is a fitted compromise between the theoretical linear scaling (volume $\propto \sigma$) and the empirical $\sqrt{\sigma}$ saturation in mature pools.

**Time-in-range.** Under geometric Brownian motion (GBM) with drift $\mu$ and vol $\sigma$, the probability that $\log p_s/p_t$ remains in $[-w\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H},\,+w\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}]$ for all $s \in [t, t+H]$ admits the reflection-principle closed form

$$
\mathbb{P}\bigl[\,\tau_w > H\,\bigr] \;=\; \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \Bigl[\Phi(\,(2n+1)w - \tfrac{\mu}{\sigma}\sqrt{H}\,) - \Phi(\,(2n-1)w - \tfrac{\mu}{\sigma}\sqrt{H}\,)\Bigr]\,(-1)^n,
$$

and the *expected fraction of time in range* over $[t, t+H]$ is

$$
\Theta(w; H) \;=\; \frac{1}{H}\,\mathbb{E}\int_t^{t+H} \mathbf{1}\lbrace |\log p_s/p_t| \le w\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}\rbrace \,ds.
$$

A useful closed-form approximation for the GBM-driven occupation time in $[-w, +w]$ on the log-price axis (driftless case, $\mu=0$) follows from the spectral expansion of the heat kernel on $(-w, w)$ with absorbing boundaries:

$$
\Theta(w; H) \;\approx\; 1 - \frac{8}{\pi^2}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(2k+1)^2}\,\exp\left(-\frac{(2k+1)^2\pi^2}{8 w^2}\right).
$$

For tight ranges ($w \lesssim 1$), the first term dominates and yields the empirically useful approximation $\Theta(w) \approx 1 - (8/\pi^2)\exp(-\pi^2/(8w^2))$. For wide ranges ($w \gtrsim 2$), $\Theta \to 1$ exponentially fast. The booster effectively learns a *conditional* version of $\Theta$ where the conditioning information includes momentum, trend, and microstructure features - features that violate the unconditional GBM assumption and shift $\Theta(w)$ up or down in a state-dependent manner.

**Naive width optimum.** Aggregating fee and LVR into an expected $H$-bar net carry (ignoring IL endpoint), one obtains the stylized maximization

$$
\max_w \;\; \underbrace{\phi\,V\,\Theta(w; H)\,\mathcal{C}(w)}_{\text{fees}} \;-\; \underbrace{\tfrac18\,\hat\sigma^2\,\Theta(w; H)\,\mathcal{C}(w)}_{\text{LVR}} \;-\; \gamma\,\mathbb{P}[\tau_w \le H]\,(g + \phi_s |\Delta x|)
$$

with $\gamma$ the gas-amortization factor. The first two terms scale together in $\mathcal{C}(w)\,\Theta(w)$; the third penalizes too-narrow widths. Differentiating in $w$ and setting to zero:

$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial w}\bigl[\Theta(w)\,\mathcal{C}(w)\bigr] \;=\; -\,\gamma\,\frac{g + \phi_s|\Delta x|}{\phi V - \tfrac18\hat\sigma^2}\,\frac{\partial\,\mathbb{P}[\tau_w \le H]}{\partial w}.
$$

The left-hand side has a single interior maximum because $\Theta$ is monotone increasing and $\mathcal{C}$ monotone decreasing in $w$; the right-hand side is positive (rebalance probability decreases with $w$). The optimum $w^\star$ shifts wider for high $\hat\sigma$ and high gas, tighter for high $\phi V$. This is the *signal* the booster is trying to learn directly from features, rather than via this stylized closed form.

### §8.6 Label: net alpha vs. HODL

The CL labeling routine `compute_cl_labels` (`cl.rs:96-198`) constructs the *realized* $H$-bar net alpha of a centered default-width LP by simulating both fee accrual and IL trajectory:

$$
\boxed{
\alpha^{\text{CL}}_t \;=\; \frac{f_{t,t+H}\cdot \dfrac{v(p_{t+H}; p_l, p_h)}{v(p_t; p_l, p_h)}}{p_{t+H}/p_t} \;-\; 1,
}
$$

where

$$
f_{t,t+H} \;=\; \prod_{j=t+1}^{t+H} \Bigl[\,1 + \mathbf{1}_{\lbrace p_j \in [p_l, p_h]\rbrace }\bigl(\Delta f_j - \Delta\ell_j\bigr)\Bigr],
$$

$\Delta f_j$ given by §8.5 and $\Delta\ell_j = (\tfrac18\sigma_j^{(\text{lvr})})^2\,p_j^{1/2}\,\mathcal{C}_j\,/\,(L_{\text{ref}}\,B_y)$ the LVR delta. The denominator $p_{t+H}/p_t$ is the buy-and-hold benchmark in token1.

**Label encoding** (`cl.rs:188-194`):

$$
y_t \;=\;
\begin{cases}
\operatorname{clamp}(\alpha^{\text{CL}}_t,\,-0.05,\,+0.05) & \text{regression mode} \\[4pt]
\mathbf{1}\lbrace \alpha^{\text{CL}}_t > \theta\rbrace  & \text{classification mode},\;\theta = \text{label\_threshold}.
\end{cases}
$$

The $\pm 5\%$ regression clamp suppresses outlier folds where a single mean-reversion event distorts the gradient direction; the classification threshold $\theta = 0.001$ (per project memory) decouples the *label* from any signal-strength scaling - conviction is recovered separately at inference via predicted probability.

### §8.7 Boosting target

The CL pipeline reuses `walk_forward_signals_labeled` (`trainer.rs:787`) - the identical walk-forward GBDT engine used for LO/SO - with the only differences being the externally-provided label vector and a CL-flavored `FitnessConfig` (`cl_fitness_config`, `cl.rs:514`). In the current production wiring, the model emits a *scalar* signal $s_t \in \lbrace -1, 0, +1\rbrace$ (regression $\to$ sign by entry threshold; classification $\to$ thresholded probability), and the backtest deterministically maps $s_t$ to the width multiplier $\kappa_{s_t} \in \lbrace \kappa_{\text{wide}}, 1, \kappa_{\text{tight}}\rbrace$.

The natural multi-output extension - and the analytic target for the next pipeline iteration - is to predict $\sigma$-normalized half-widths directly. **Status: planned, not in master.** The current source code (`cl.rs:208-464`) implements only the discrete-signal mapping above; the σ-normalized form below describes the design target for the next pipeline iteration.

$$
(\hat w_l,\, \hat w_h,\, \hat\mu) \;=\; \mathcal{F}_\theta(x_t),
$$

with the realized range

$$
\boxed{
p_l \;=\; p_t\,\exp\bigl((-\hat w_l + \hat\mu)\,\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}\bigr), \qquad
p_h \;=\; p_t\,\exp\bigl((+\hat w_h + \hat\mu)\,\hat\sigma_P\sqrt{H}\bigr),
}
$$

trained against the empirical $\alpha^{\text{CL}}$ surface evaluated on a discrete grid of candidate $(w_l, w_h, \mu)$ per fold. The booster is the same `prime_ml::booster` perpetual GBDT used for directional prediction; only the label tensor is multidimensional. Folds inherit the same embargo $E = H + \Delta_{\text{eval}}$ and the same train-stride throttle ($\le 7000$ rows per fit) used by the LO/SO path.

**Three estimation strategies, in order of statistical efficiency.**

1. **Direct regression on argmax.** For each fold-row $t$, evaluate $\alpha^{\text{CL}}_t(w_l, w_h, \mu)$ on a small grid (e.g. $5\times 5\times 3 = 75$ points) and take

$$
y_t \;=\; \arg\max_{(w_l, w_h, \mu)}\; \alpha^{\text{CL}}_t(w_l, w_h, \mu).
$$

Cheap to label but the argmax is high-variance - many rows have a near-flat $\alpha$ surface and the booster learns label noise.

2. **Multi-task regression on the full $\alpha$ surface.** Fit three boosters $\mathcal{F}^{w_l}_\theta, \mathcal{F}^{w_h}_\theta, \mathcal{F}^{\mu}_\theta$ each regressing on the smoothed-argmax target with confidence-weighted training rows: row weight $\propto \alpha^{\text{CL}}_t(\text{argmax}) - \alpha^{\text{CL}}_t(\text{default})$, so the model focuses on rows where range choice matters.

3. **Policy-gradient on $\alpha^{\text{CL}}$ directly.** Treat $\mathcal{F}_\theta$ as a deterministic policy and differentiate the realized $\alpha^{\text{CL}}$ trajectory by autograd through the LP value function (continuous everywhere except at the range boundaries). This sidesteps label construction entirely but requires a differentiable simulator - a candidate workstream once the simpler approaches are calibrated.

The fitness layer in `cl_fitness_config` (`cl.rs:514-551`) overrides the global default to encode the asymmetric loss structure appropriate for CL. In particular, the trajectory weight is raised from the LO/SO default $w_\text{traj} = 0.30$ (`fitness.rs:152`) to a CL-specific $w_\text{traj} = 0.40$ (`cl.rs:542`), with the corresponding compensating reduction in $w_{\text{win\_rate}}$ from $0.15$ to $0.05$ (`cl.rs:549`); this elevates equity-curve quality over total-return realization because a perpetual LP is judged on its drawdown profile rather than its trade-by-trade hit rate. The profit-factor floor is relaxed ($pf_{\text{baseline}} = 0.8$, no minimum) because legitimate CL strategies routinely experience extended IL-dominated drawdowns that should not disqualify a fold. Drawdown duration is penalized with a $15$-day target and a $10$-day half-life, capturing the operational reality that an LP underwater for a quarter is functionally indistinguishable from one that exited.

### §8.8 Connection to the ALM vault

The off-chain output $(p_l, p_h)$ is mapped to integer ticks via §8.3 and emitted to the ALM keeper, which calls the on-chain adapter at `~/Work/btr/alm/evm/src/adapters/Dex.sol`. The relevant state is

```solidity
int24 public tickLower;
int24 public tickUpper;
event Rebalanced(int24 tickLower, int24 tickUpper, uint128 liquidity);
```

The keeper sequence is: (i) burn the current position via `pool.burn(tickLower, tickUpper, L)`; (ii) collect accumulated fees; (iii) swap the resulting unbalanced inventory back toward 50/50 using the swap-fraction formula

$$
\xi(p; p_l, p_h) \;=\; \left|\,\frac{\sqrt{p} - \sqrt{p_l}}{(\sqrt{p} - \sqrt{p_l}) + (\sqrt{p} - p/\sqrt{p_h})} - \tfrac12\,\right|
$$

(implemented in `compute_swap_fraction`, `cl.rs:477-491`), which equals $0.5$ when out of range and $\to 0$ as $p \to \sqrt{p_l p_h}$; (iv) re-mint at the new ticks $(T_l^\star, T_h^\star)$. The on-chain `Rebalanced` event is the canonical confirmation Prime consumes to advance `last_rebal_bar`.

### §8.9 Cost model and rebalance throttle

Rebalance cost per event:

$$
\mathcal{K}_t \;=\; \underbrace{g}_{\text{gas USD}} \;+\; \underbrace{\phi_s \cdot \xi_t \cdot V_t^{\text{LP}}}_{\text{swap fee on the imbalanced leg}},
$$

with $g = 0.15$ USD (L2-tier gas) and $\phi_s = 5$ bps (Uniswap 0.05% tier) per `CLMLConfig::default`. The throttle has two layers:

1. **Hard cooldown.** $i_t - i_{\text{last rebal}} \ge \Delta_{\min} = 48$ bars (`min_rebal_gap`), preventing fee-flicker oscillation.
2. **Divergence trigger.** Rebalance fires only when the *interval divergence* between current $(p_l, p_h)$ and target $(\tilde p_l, \tilde p_h)$ exceeds $\theta_d = 0.35$:

$$
D \;=\; \min\left(\frac{\bigl|(\tilde p_h - \tilde p_l) - (p_h - p_l)\bigr|}{p_h - p_l} \;+\; \frac{|\tilde c - c|}{p_h - p_l},\; 1\right),
\qquad c = \tfrac{p_l + p_h}{2}.
$$

This is `raw_divergence` in `range.rs:88-96`. A minimum-improvement test in expected-α space - i.e. only rebalance if $\mathbb{E}[\alpha^{\text{CL}}\,|\,\text{new range}] - \mathbb{E}[\alpha^{\text{CL}}\,|\,\text{old range}] > \mathcal{K}_t / V_t^{\text{LP}}$ - is not yet present in the source; the current heuristic is the divergence threshold acting as a proxy.
