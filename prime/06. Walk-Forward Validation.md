# Walk-Forward Validation


### §6.1 Walk-forward setup

BTR Prime trains the gradient-boosted machine (GBM) under a **rolling-origin walk-forward** harness whose units are *information events* (bars), not wall-clock time. The pivotal design choice is that the renko / DIB bar stream is irregular in time but regular in information content, so quantities measured per-bar are stationary in $\sigma$-units even when calendar duration is not. Configuration (`config.yml`, `WalkForwardConfig::default`):

$$
\begin{aligned}
W_{\text{train}} &= 5000\ \text{bars} \quad\text{(`train-bars`)} \\
S &= 500\ \text{bars} \quad\text{(`step-bars` / `retrain-bars`)} \\
H &= 20\ \text{bars} \quad\text{(prediction horizon, `horizon-bars`)} \\
\Delta_{\text{eval}} &= 20\ \text{bars} \quad\text{(signal cadence, `eval-interval`)}
\end{aligned}
$$

Let $\lbrace b_i\rbrace_{i=0}^{N-1}$ be the ordered bar stream, with timestamps $t_i = \mathrm{ts}(b_i)$. Define fold $k \in \lbrace 0, 1, 2, \dots\rbrace$ by the cursor sequence

$$
c_k \;=\; W_{\text{train}} + kS, \qquad k=0,1,\dots, \quad \text{while } c_k + E < N,
$$

where the embargo $E$ is defined in §6.2. The training window, prediction window, and refit point for fold $k$ are

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{T}_k &= \bigl[\,c_k - W_{\text{train}},\; c_k - H\,\bigr) && \text{(train indices)} \\
\mathcal{P}_k &= \bigl[\,c_k + E,\; \min(c_k + S + E,\, N)\,\bigr) && \text{(predict / OOS indices)} \\
c_{k+1} &= c_k + S && \text{(retrain step)}
\end{aligned}
$$

Predictions are emitted every $\Delta_{\text{eval}}$ bars within $\mathcal{P}_k$. Folds therefore overlap on training but tile OOS: $\bigsqcup_k \mathcal{P}_k$ is a disjoint cover of the evaluation universe. The realized number of folds is $K \approx (N - W_{\text{train}}) / S$.

**Theorem 6.1 (Information-agnostic stationarity).** If features $X_i$ and labels $y_i$ are measurable functions of the bar stream restricted to a fixed *bar* window, the empirical distribution of $(X_i, y_i)$ within fold $k$ is invariant to the calendar duration of $\mathcal{T}_k$. Hence the walk-forward harness preserves the statistical regularity of renko / DIB events even though $\Delta t_i$ is random. *Proof sketch.* Bar construction is a stopping-time transform of price: a renko brick closes at the first tick $\tau$ such that $|p_\tau - p_{\text{open}}| \ge \delta$; a DIB bar closes at the first $\tau$ such that the cumulative signed dollar volume exceeds an adaptive threshold $\theta_t$. In either case conditioning on the event count fixes the information sigma-algebra independent of $\Delta t$. The features $X_i$ are then evaluated on $(p, v, ts)$ within the closed bar, all of which are constructions independent of calendar time. $\square$

**Practical consequence.** $W_{\text{train}} = 5000$ renko bars at the BTC brick size used in production corresponds to roughly $5{-}9$ calendar days during regime $A$ (high vol) but $30{-}60$ days during regime $B$ (low vol). The walk-forward harness is therefore *self-adapting in calendar time*: more frequent retrains in volatile markets, slower retrains in quiet ones, while the *information content* of each fold stays constant. This is the explicit point of the bar-not-time abstraction noted in the trainer doc-comment (`trainer.rs:7-10`).

Source: `trainer.rs:99-135` (defaults), `trainer.rs:435-441` (`walk_forward_ml_sliced` signature), `trainer.rs:516-703` (main loop).

### §6.2 No-look-ahead invariants

Three invariants jointly eliminate label leakage. Adopting López de Prado (2018, ch. 7) terminology:

**(I1) Feature causality.** The feature row $X_i$ at bar $i$ is a measurable function of $\lbrace b_j : j \le i\rbrace$. The codebase enforces this by building the feature matrix once via `precompute_data(...)` over the full series and indexing strictly by `train_indices` / `pred_indices` (`trainer.rs:524-533`, `trainer.rs:597-604`).

> **Caveat - feature *selection* is global.** Per-row causality holds, but the *selection* of which features enter the matrix (variance-pruning, NaN-screening, cross-asset alignment) is performed **once** inside `precompute_data` over the full series, not re-run per fold. This is a known in-sample step: it does not leak future values into a row but it does mean the *feature set* itself was chosen with awareness of the full sample. The leakage magnitude is small for the stationary feature families used here (Hurst, RSI, MTF), but the practice is non-canonical relative to a strict López-de-Prado purged-CV; tracked in [§10 Open Questions §6](./10.%20Open%20Questions.md).

**(I2) Label purging.** Labels are forward returns

$$
y_i \;=\; \begin{cases}
\mathbf{1}\left\lbrace\,\frac{c_{i+H}}{c_i}-1 > \tau\,\right\rbrace & \text{(classification, } \tau=0.001\text{)} \\[4pt]
\operatorname{clip}_{\pm 5}\Big(\tfrac{(c_{i+H}/c_i - 1)}{\sigma_P(i)\sqrt{H}}\Big) & \text{(regression, Parkinson-normalized)}
\end{cases}
$$

where $c_i$ is the bar close and $\sigma_P$ the rolling Parkinson estimator (`parkinson_lookback = 50`). Because $y_i$ depends on $\lbrace c_{i+1},\dots,c_{i+H}\rbrace$, the training window is truncated to $[c_k - W_{\text{train}},\, c_k - H)$ - i.e. *purged* of any bar whose label leaks into the embargo zone. See `trainer.rs:517`:
```rust
let train_end = cursor.saturating_sub(cfg.horizon_bars);
```

**(I3) Embargo.** Beyond purging, an explicit embargo of $E$ bars separates the trained model from its first prediction. The total embargo length decomposes into two *independent* requirements that happen to add:

**(a) Purge horizon $E_{\text{purge}} = H$.** Required because the label $y_i$ at time $i$ is a measurable function of bars $(i, i+H]$. If any bar $i$ with $i + H > c_k - 1$ were retained in the training set, the model would receive a label whose realization window touches the prediction region, creating direct leakage of future price information into the fit. The minimal purge is therefore $E \ge H$.

**(b) Inter-fold disjointness $E_{\text{disj}} = \Delta_{\text{eval}}$.** Required because predictions in $\mathcal{P}_k$ are emitted on a grid with stride $\Delta_{\text{eval}}$, and the next fold's training window $\mathcal{T}_{k+1}$ begins at $c_{k+1} - W_{\text{train}} = c_k + S - W_{\text{train}}$. Without a $\Delta_{\text{eval}}$ buffer, the last eval bar of fold $k$ (whose features depend on bars up to and including itself) could be re-used as a training input for fold $k+1$ - its label would then be derived from the same forward window the previous fold *predicted on*, contaminating the next training set with information used in the prior fold's OOS evaluation.

**Total:**

$$
E \;=\; E_{\text{purge}} + E_{\text{disj}} \;=\; H + \Delta_{\text{eval}} \;=\; 20 + 20 \;=\; 40\ \text{bars}\quad(\text{`trainer.rs:495`}).
$$

The two terms are independent: $E_{\text{purge}}$ addresses *label-window leakage* within a single fold's training cut; $E_{\text{disj}}$ addresses *cross-fold contamination* of training inputs between consecutive folds. Either alone is insufficient.

Formally, let $\mathcal{F}_i^X = \sigma(\lbrace X_j : j \le i\rbrace )$ and $\mathcal{F}_i^y = \sigma(\lbrace y_j : j+H \le i, y_j \text{ measurable at } i\rbrace )$. The walk-forward predictor at $c_k + E$ is measurable w.r.t.

$$
\mathcal{G}_k \;=\; \mathcal{F}_{c_k - H}^{X} \cap \mathcal{F}_{c_k - H}^{y},
$$

i.e. it strictly precedes every realization in $\mathcal{P}_k$. Cite López de Prado 2018, §7.4 (purging) and §7.4.2 (embargo).

### §6.3 Out-of-sample metrics

Let $\lbrace e_t\rbrace$ denote the realized equity curve sampled on the eval grid (timestamp, equity). Per-bar arithmetic returns are

$$
r_t \;=\; \frac{e_{t}}{e_{t-1}} - 1,
$$

and zero-return idle bars are discarded (`compute_risk_metrics`, `trainer.rs:1326-1329`) to prevent inflating Sharpe/Sortino from inactivity.

**Annualization.** Let $B$ be `bars_per_day_for_annualization` (default $1440$ for 1-min bars, override for renko via realized bars/day). Eval-grid samples per year:

> **⚠ Renko caveat.** The default $B = 1440$ assumes 1-minute calendar bars. Adaptive-Renko bars fire at an *event-driven* rate that depends on realised volatility and is typically far below 1440/day. If the override is not supplied, the annualised Sharpe is inflated by $\sqrt{1440 / B_{\text{actual}}}$ (e.g. ≈$\sqrt{14.4} \approx 3.8\times$ for a 100-bar/day Renko stream). Always supply the realised bars/day for the production stream; tracked in [§10 Open Questions §6](./10.%20Open%20Questions.md).


$$
N_{\text{yr}} \;=\; \frac{B}{\Delta_{\text{eval}}} \cdot 252 \quad \text{(trading-day convention)},
$$

or $\frac{B}{\Delta_{\text{eval}}} \cdot 365.25$ in the calendar-day convention used by `monthly_geo_sharpe` (`trainer.rs:1339`).

**Sharpe (monthly-geometric).** Rather than the naive $\sqrt{N_{\text{yr}}}\,\bar r / \sigma_r$, BTR uses

$$
\widehat{\mathrm{SR}}_{\text{geo}} \;=\; \exp\Bigl(\tfrac{1}{M}\sum_{m=1}^{M}\ln\max\bigl(\mathrm{SR}_m,\,\varepsilon\bigr)\Bigr),
\quad
\mathrm{SR}_m \;=\; \sqrt{N_{\text{yr}}}\,\frac{\bar r_m}{\sigma_{r,m}},
$$

the geometric mean of monthly annualized Sharpes ($M$ months in series). This penalizes one-shot outlier months that would game the naive ratio.

**Sortino.** Same construction with downside-only second moment:

$$
\sigma^{-}_{r,m} \;=\; \sqrt{\tfrac{1}{|\mathcal{M}_m|}\sum_{t \in \mathcal{M}_m}r_t^2\,\mathbf{1}\lbrace r_t<0\rbrace }, \quad
\widehat{\mathrm{Sortino}}_m \;=\; \sqrt{N_{\text{yr}}}\,\bar r_m / \sigma^{-}_{r,m}.
$$

**Max drawdown.** Continuous-time:

$$
\mathrm{MDD} \;=\; \max_{t}\,\frac{\max_{s \le t} e_s - e_t}{\max_{s \le t} e_s}\,.
$$

Implemented as a single pass with running peak (`trainer.rs:1343-1349`).

**Hit rate.** $\mathrm{WR} = |\lbrace i : \mathrm{pnl}_i > 0\rbrace | / N_{\text{trades}}$.

**Profit factor.** $\mathrm{PF} = \sum \mathrm{pnl}^+ / \sum |\mathrm{pnl}^-|$.

**Trajectory $t$-statistic.** Fit OLS on $\ln e_t$ vs $t$:

$$
\ln e_t \;=\; \alpha + \beta\, t + \epsilon_t, \quad t\text{-stat} \;=\; \hat\beta / \mathrm{SE}(\hat\beta),\quad R^2 \in [0,1].
$$

This is the *dominant* component of the fitness function (§6.4). High slope alone is insufficient - a non-linear curve has high $\beta$ but low $R^2$ and is penalized.

### §6.4 Fitness function

The optimization objective (`fitness.rs::compute_fitness`) is a **weighted geometric mean of bounded sub-scores**, multiplied by a confidence discount tied to trade count. Hard filters preempt scoring; soft sub-scores are continuous in $[0.01, 1]$ to avoid flat gradient regions for optimizers.

**Hard filters (Phase 1, `fitness.rs:266-354`):** the candidate is set to $\text{fitness} = 0$ if any of

$$
\begin{aligned}
N_{\text{trades}} &< 20, \\
\mathrm{cov} &= \tfrac{|\lbrace d : \exists i, \lfloor (t_i - t_0)/86400000 \rfloor = d\rbrace |}{\lceil \mathrm{span}_{\text{days}} \rceil} < 0.20, \\
\mathrm{MDD} &\ge 25\%, \\
\mathrm{PF} &< 1.0, \\
\widehat{\mathrm{Sortino}} &< 0, \\
N_{\text{trades}} &< \lceil \mathrm{span}_{\text{wk}} \cdot 2 \rceil, \quad\text{(load-bearing gate)} \\
\tfrac{|\lbrace i : 5\text{min} \le \text{hold}_i \le 30\text{d}\rbrace |}{N_{\text{trades}}} &< 0.5.
\end{aligned}
$$

Per the comment at `fitness.rs:124`, the absolute `min_trades = 20` is an *absolute floor* (sanity gate against degenerate runs), while `min_trades_per_week = 2.0` - i.e. $N_{\text{trades}} < \lceil \mathrm{span}_{\text{wk}} \cdot 2 \rceil$ - is the *load-bearing* statistical-power gate that scales with sample span. For runs spanning $> 10$ weeks the per-week gate dominates the absolute floor.

**Soft sub-scores (Phases 3–7.6).** Let $\sigma_{\text{traj}} = 3$, $\sigma_{\text{Sort}} = 2$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
s_{\text{traj}} &= \max\bigl(0,\,\tanh(t\text{-stat}/\sigma_{\text{traj}})\bigr)\cdot R^2 \\[3pt]
s_{\text{dd}} &= \exp\bigl(-\bar D_{\text{mo}} / d_{\text{band}}\bigr),\quad d_{\text{band}}=8\% \\[3pt]
s_{\text{ddur}} &= \exp\bigl(-\bar T_{\text{epi}} / T_e\bigr),\quad T_e=16\ \text{d} \\[3pt]
s_{\text{tail}} &= \bigl(1 + (\mathrm{ES\_ratio}/1.5)^2\bigr)^{-1} \\[3pt]
s_{\text{pf}} &= \max\bigl(0.01,\,\tanh\tfrac{\mathrm{PF}_{\text{geo}} - 1}{0.4}\bigr),\quad \mathrm{PF}_{\text{geo}} \le 3 \\[3pt]
s_{\text{cov}} &= \max\bigl(0,\,\tanh(\mathrm{cov}/0.5)\bigr) \\[3pt]
s_{\text{wr}} &= \max\bigl(0.01,\,\tanh\tfrac{\mathrm{WR}-0.20}{0.15}\bigr)
\end{aligned}
$$

where:
- $\bar D_{\text{mo}}$ = geometric mean of per-month max drawdowns (`monthly_avg_max_dd`, `fitness.rs:680-703`).
- $\bar T_{\text{epi}}$ = geometric mean of underwater-episode durations measured against the global high-water mark (`avg_drawdown_episode_days`, `fitness.rs:708-738`).
- $T_e = 16$ d is the **e-fold time** of the drawdown-duration penalty (not a half-life): $s_{\text{ddur}}$ falls to $1/e$ at $\bar T_{\text{epi}} = T_e$. The configuration field is named `dd_duration_halflife_days` for backward compatibility; the actual half-life is $T_e \cdot \ln 2 \approx 11.1$ d. The misnaming is a known doc/code drift - see [§10 Open Questions §1](./10.%20Open%20Questions.md).
- $\mathrm{ES\_ratio}$ = geometric mean of monthly $|\mathrm{CVaR}_{0.95}|/\sigma$ ratios (`monthly_avg_cvar`, `fitness.rs:819-868`).
- $\mathrm{PF}_{\text{geo}}$ = geometric mean of monthly profit factors capped at 3.0 (`monthly_geo_profit_factor`, `fitness.rs:775-815`).

**Aggregate (Phase 8, weighted geometric mean):** with weights $w$ satisfying $\sum w_j = 1$ (verified by unit test `fitness.rs:875-881`),

$$
\text{raw-fit} \;=\; \prod_{j} s_j^{w_j} \;=\; s_{\text{traj}}^{0.30}\,s_{\text{dd}}^{0.10}\,s_{\text{ddur}}^{0.10}\,s_{\text{tail}}^{0.10}\,s_{\text{pf}}^{0.10}\,s_{\text{cov}}^{0.15}\,s_{\text{wr}}^{0.15}.
$$

The Sortino-derived return score $s_{\text{ret}} = \max(0, \tanh(\widehat{\mathrm{Sortino}}/2))$ is computed for diagnostics but carries weight $w_{\text{ret}} = 0$ (display-only) - return signal enters indirectly through the trajectory $t$-stat.

**Confidence discount (Phase 9):**

$$
\text{conf} \;=\; 0.5 + 0.5\,\sqrt{\min(N_{\text{trades}}/200,\,1)} \;\in\; [0.5, 1.0],
$$

$$
\boxed{\;\text{fitness} \;=\; \text{conf}\cdot\prod_{j} s_j^{w_j}\;.}
$$

**Theorem 6.2 (Geometric-mean veto suppression).** For $s_j \in (0,1]$ and $w_j > 0$, the gradient $\partial \log(\text{raw-fit}) / \partial \log s_j = w_j$ is bounded - no single sub-score can fully veto, but each has proportional influence. Hard zero is reached iff $s_j \downarrow 0$ for some $j$, which the $0.01$ floors prevent except via filter rejection. *Justification of design:* the additive log-form encodes priority via $w_j$ while avoiding the brittle plateaus of `min()`-style multi-objective gating. $\square$

### §6.5 Cost model

Per-side proportional fee `fee_rate_bps = 3.0` (`config.yml:` not exposed, default `trainer.rs:124`). For a position of notional size $\$S$ entered at $p_{\text{in}}$ and exited at $p_{\text{out}}$ with leverage $L$ and direction $d \in \lbrace \pm 1\rbrace$ (`trainer.rs:1057-1080`):

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{pnl}_{\text{gross}} &= S \cdot L \cdot d \cdot \bigl(p_{\text{out}}/p_{\text{in}} - 1\bigr), \\
\mathrm{fee}_{\text{in}} &= S \cdot \phi, \\
\mathrm{fee}_{\text{out}} &= S \cdot \max\bigl(1 + \mathrm{pnl}_{\text{gross}}/(SL),\,0\bigr)\cdot \phi, \quad \phi = 3\text{bps} = 3\times10^{-4}, \\
\mathrm{pnl}_{\text{net}} &= \mathrm{pnl}_{\text{gross}} - \mathrm{fee}_{\text{in}} - \mathrm{fee}_{\text{out}}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Note that the exit-fee notional grows with realized PnL (correct treatment for a margined perpetual), and clamps to zero for full-wipeout losses. **Slippage is *not* explicitly modeled** in the current backtester - execution is at signal-bar `close`, with intra-bar SL/TP hit-detection at the wick extremes (high/low) of subsequent bars (`trainer.rs:1097-1118`). This makes the SL/TP fills slightly *adverse* (fill at the trigger, not the wick), giving a mild conservative bias against the strategy. No funding/borrow rate is applied; leverage is treated as a notional multiplier with no carry cost. Tracked in [Open Questions §2](./10.%20Open%20Questions.md): add linear / $\sqrt{q}$ impact when keeper deployment exposes realized fill-vs-mid slippage.

**Position sizing.** Two scalings shrink the base size beyond the fixed $\$S_0 = \text{capital}\cdot\rho$, $\rho = 0.15$:

$$
S \;=\; S_0 \cdot \underbrace{\operatorname{clip}\bigl(\tfrac{|\hat y|}{\tau_\sigma},\,0.2,\,1.0\bigr)}_{\text{conviction scale}} \cdot \underbrace{\max\Bigl(0.3,\; 1 - 0.7\,\min(\mathrm{DD}/0.12,\,1)\Bigr)}_{\text{drawdown scale}},
$$

where $\tau_\sigma =$ `entry_threshold_sigma = 0.20` and $\mathrm{DD}$ is current drawdown from peak (`trainer.rs:1220-1236`). Drawdown scale floor at $0.3$ keeps the strategy alive through stress; conviction scale floor at $0.2$ prevents zero-size signals from cluttering the trade ledger. The compounding switch `cfg.compound = false` in production means $S_0$ is computed against *initial* capital, decoupling sizing from realized PnL - this stabilizes statistical comparison across folds and walks (`trainer.rs:1235`).

### §6.6 Stage-1 vs Stage-2 (meta-labeling) separation

The trainer reserves the López de Prado two-stage architecture but **currently runs only Stage 1** in master. The `FoldModels` struct (`trainer.rs:203-208`) declares an optional `meta_model: Option<GBMModel>` slot that is populated as `None` throughout the walk-forward loops (e.g. `trainer.rs:587`, `trainer.rs:888`). When activated, meta-labeling will operate as follows under the same purge/embargo invariants:

1. **Stage-1 train** on $\mathcal{T}_k$ → primary GBM predicting $\mathrm{sign}(y_i)$ or $y_i/(\sigma_P\sqrt H)$.
2. **Stage-1 inference on a held-out *meta fold***: predictions on $\mathcal{T}_k^{\text{meta}} = [\,c_k - W_{\text{train}}/2,\; c_k - H\,)$ are *out-of-sample relative to the meta classifier* because the meta classifier is trained on the same fold's stage-1 model but evaluated on its own labels.
3. **Meta-labels** are binary "would Stage-1's bet at $i$ have won net of costs?" These are produced *from the trade simulator* using stage-1 predictions, the same SL/TP rules, and a fee-net PnL.
4. **Stage-2 train** on (features ∪ stage-1 prediction) → $\mathcal{T}_k^{\text{meta}}$ with binary meta-labels.
5. **Inference on $\mathcal{P}_k$:** apply meta probability $m_i$ as a *gate* (`signal = 1` iff $m_i > p^\star$) and/or *size-multiplier* ($\text{size} \propto m_i$).

The critical leakage failure mode is using stage-1's *in-sample* predictions on $\mathcal{T}_k$ as features for stage-2 - these are over-fit and produce optimistic meta-labels. The correct discipline (and the one the codebase is structured to support) is **nested cross-fitting**: split $\mathcal{T}_k$ into two halves, train stage-1 on one half, predict on the other, swap, concatenate to form stage-2 training data. The embargo $E = H + \Delta_{\text{eval}}$ continues to apply at the inner split. Until meta-labeling is wired in, fitness is reported on raw stage-1 trades only.
