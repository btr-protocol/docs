# 7. Operator Doctrine

> Who can do what, when, and how the contracts enforce the separation.

---

## 1. Roles

| Role | Source | Privileges |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Owner** | `AccessControl.owner()` | Full control of governance -fees, knobs, kill switch, adapter add/remove, salvage, factory/treasury/swapper queueing. |
| **Keeper** | `AccessControl.isKeeper[a]` | Defensive ratchets, kill (rate-limited + capped), allocate/collectFrom, rebalance, harvestPerformance. |
| **Adapter** | `AccessControl.isAdapter[a]` | Charges keeper-kill against the cluster cap (registered by Factory only). |
| **Holder** | `Dex.isHolder[a]` | Calls `pull` / `withdraw` on a Dex adapter. Vaults are holders; EOAs / multisigs may also be holders. |
| **Operator** (per-controller) | `Vault.isOperator[ctrl][a]` | Standing approval to call `redeem` / `requestRedeem` / `claimRedeem` for a specific controller (ERC-7540). |

## 3. Owner-or-Keeper Actions

`Vault`:

- `setConfig("depositCap", v)` -keeper may only ratchet **down** and never below `totalAssets()` (`Vault.sol:setConfig`, ≈ L412).
- `allocate` / `collectFrom` (`Vault.sol:allocate` ≈ L521, `:collectFrom` ≈ L531, `:collectFromLp` ≈ L551).
- `harvestPerformance` (`Vault.sol:harvestPerformance`, ≈ L441).

`Dex`:

- `setConfig("maxSlipBps", v)` -narrow-only on both legs since Pass-15 P15-1: keeper may only ratchet **down** (no per-call rate-limit; intentional emergency surface), and owner widen reverts NotAuth, so owner must stage widens through `queueSlip`/`executeSlip` (Pass-17 P17-1 + Pass-19 P19-1 tighten the queue cap to `JOINT_SLIP_CAP_BPS - ADAPTER_SLIP_HEADROOM_BPS` = 700 bps to cover the worst-case `Vault.MAX_EXIT_PIP / 10 = 300 bps` `exitBps` term; Pass-21 P21-1 corrected the constant `MAX_EXIT_PIP` from `30_000` → `3_000` pip so the headroom is exact-fit, not 10× under-sized).
- `setConfig("minIntervalSecs", v)` -keeper may only ratchet **up**, by at most `KEEPER_INTERVAL_DELTA = 1 day` per call (`Dex.sol:setConfig`, ≈ L220).
- `kill()` -keeper rate-limited (`KILL_RATE_LIMIT = 1 hour`) + cluster cap (`AC.chargeKeeperKill`, daily cap = 2).
- `rebalance` (`Dex.sol:rebalance`, ≈ L402).

The pattern: **keepers can only move risk knobs in the safer direction**. The owner is the only authority that can loosen them -and freeze-trigger params (`deviationBps`, `heartbeatDriftBps`) are timelocked even for the owner.

## 6. Defensive Ratchets (Keeper Direction)

| Knob | Permitted keeper direction |
|------|----------------------------|
| `Vault.depositCap` | ↓ only, ≥ `totalAssets()` |
| `Dex.maxSlipBps` | ↓ only (owner-loosen via `queueSlip`; cap = `ADAPTER_SLIP_CAP_BPS = 700 bps` per Pass-19 P19-1) |
| `BtrPoolAdapter.maxSlipBps` | ↓ only (owner-loosen via `queueSlip`; cap = `ADAPTER_SLIP_CAP_BPS = 700 bps` per Pass-19 P19-1) |
| `Dex.minIntervalSecs` | ↑ only, ≤ current + `KEEPER_INTERVAL_DELTA` |

The principle: a keeper can always make the system **more** defensive. Loosening always requires the owner.

**Pass-19 P19-1**: adapter-side `queueSlip` cap = `JOINT_SLIP_CAP_BPS - ADAPTER_SLIP_HEADROOM_BPS = 1000 - 300 = 700 bps`. The 300 bps headroom is sized ≥ `Vault.MAX_EXIT_PIP / 10 = 3_000 / 10 = 300 bps` (Pass-21 P21-1 fixed `MAX_EXIT_PIP` from `30_000` → `3_000` pip = 300 bps = 3%) so even worst-case `exitPip` cannot soft-DoS `Vault._assertJointSlip`. Headroom is reserved unconditionally; zeroing exit fees does **not** unlock additional adapter slip. Pass-29 P29-4 adds compile-time invariant guards in `Constants.sol` that fail-compile if these invariants are ever broken by a constant edit (Pass-31 R31-2 tightens the headroom guard to BPS·10 vs MAX_EXIT_PIP so every +1-PIP perturbation trips compile, not just multiples of 10).

**Pass-31 R31-6 - scope of the 700 bps envelope**: the `JOINT_SLIP_CAP_BPS - ADAPTER_SLIP_HEADROOM_BPS = 700 bps` adapter cap is a **config-time bound** enforced by `_assertJointSlip` at `queueSlip`/`setConfig` time. It bounds the worst-case **per-leg execution slippage tolerance** that an honest adapter will surface on `swap`/`addLiquidity`/`removeLiquidity` paths. It does **NOT** apply to `Vault.collectFromLp`: that path takes a keeper-supplied `minOut` (Z3-F6 documented trusted-keeper contract) and is **not** re-checked against `_assertJointSlip`. Worst-case compromised-keeper `collectFromLp` loss = 100% of LP value per call. Defense surface = (a) off-chain alerting + signing-key hygiene, (b) owner `removeAdapter(a)` to evict the route, (c) 2-step ownership / handover (§8) preventing key-rotation hijack, (d) per-adapter `KILL_RATE_LIMIT = 1h` slowing keeper-initiated kill spam if the compromised keeper attempts a denial-of-service in parallel.

## 7. Daily Kill Cap

`AccessControl.chargeKeeperKill()` (`AccessControl.sol:chargeKeeperKill`, ≈ L90):

- Adapter-callable only (`isAdapter[msg.sender]` required).
- UTC-day epoch; 2 keeper kills per UTC day across the **entire cluster**.
- Owner-initiated kills bypass this cap (call adapter `kill` directly without going through AC).

Without this cap, a single keeper × 8 adapters × 1/h could freeze a vault for 8 hours. The cap forces a real human (owner) into the loop on the second incident of the day.

## 8. Two-Step Ownership

`AccessControl` overrides Solady's 1-step `transferOwnership` and `renounceOwnership` to revert (`AccessControl.sol:transferOwnership` ≈ L68, `:renounceOwnership` ≈ L69). Handover requires:

1. Current owner → `requestOwnershipHandover(newOwner)` (Solady, time-windowed).
2. New owner → `completeOwnershipHandover(pendingOwner)` (`AccessControl.sol:completeOwnershipHandover` ≈ L72, with zero-target rejection).

The zero-target rejection closes the back-door renounce vector.
