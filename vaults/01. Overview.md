# Overview

> BTR Supply is the protocol's ALM (Automated Liquidity Management) layer. Users deposit single-asset capital into vaults; the vault routes capital across adapters (3rd-party CL DEX pools today — UniV3, UniV4, PancakeV3, PancakeInfinity, Algebra, Ramses; forward-looking: BTR DEX/AIMM pools) and rebalances per strategy.

Vault shares are ERC-4626/7540 fungible receipts; Aerodrome Slipstream is served by the UniV3 adapter. NAV uses pessimistic worst-of(pool, oracle) marks; rebalances trigger on regime signals (realized volatility, pool deviation, sequencer health, intent backlog).

---

## 1. Position in the Product Stack

> For the cross-surface product matrix, see [Concepts → Overview](../concepts/overview.md).

A user holding `USDC` deposits to a vault, receives fungible vault shares (ERC-4626/7540), and **is designed to** earn net-of-fee yield from concentrated LP positions managed by the protocol. The vault chooses ranges, rebalances atomically or via ERC-7683 intents, and crystallizes performance fees on a high-water mark.

## 2. Mental Model

- **Earn-first surface**. Vaults are the passive yield product (Phase 1, live); BTR DEX (AIMM) is the future active trading venue (Phase 2). Swap is the off-chain aggregator (distinct from DEX). All surfaces share the same treasury, governance, and price-feed substrate.
- **Single-asset accounting**. Every vault is denominated in one ERC-20 (`Vault.sol:37 _asset`). LP exposure is internal to the adapter; users see only one decimal of risk: the deposit asset.
- **Worst-of marks**. Share price is pessimistic -at each adapter, `min(pool, oracle)` denominated in `asset`. JIT-extraction cost ≈ pool slippage; oracle-extraction cost ≈ Chainlink staleness. Never both.
